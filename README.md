My work is less about showing code and more about showing how AI products are shaped: what problem they solve, how the workflow behaves, how quality is evaluated, and what decisions make the product useful in the real world.

## What This Portfolio Is

This GitHub is my AI Product Management portfolio.

Instead of treating repositories as code dumps, I use them as product case studies. Each one is written to make the product thinking visible:

- the user problem
- the AI workflow
- the product decisions
- the evaluation approach
- the launch and iteration plan

The goal is simple: show how I think about building AI products.

## How I Build AI Products

| Product layer | Questions I care about |
|---|---|
| Problem | Who is this for, and what painful workflow are we improving? |
| Product | What should the user experience feel like before, during, and after the AI interaction? |
| AI system | What should the model classify, generate, evaluate, or recommend? |
| Quality | What does a good output look like, and how do we catch weak ones? |
| Operations | Where do humans review, override, or improve the system? |
| Growth | What user behavior, conversion, or retention signal tells us it is working? |

## Featured Case Study

### AI Interview Platform

![AI Evaluation](https://img.shields.io/badge/AI%20Evaluation-4D96FF?style=flat-square)
![Interview UX](https://img.shields.io/badge/Interview%20UX-FF6B6B?style=flat-square)
![Validation](https://img.shields.io/badge/Validation-6BCB77?style=flat-square)
![Prompt Strategy](https://img.shields.io/badge/Prompt%20Strategy-B983FF?style=flat-square)

An AI-powered mock interview platform for company-specific interview preparation.

The product supports technical, behavioral, resume, product sense, data, and architecture interview loops. The important product challenge was not just generating questions; it was designing a believable interview flow, evaluating answers consistently, and giving candidates feedback they could actually use.

**What I owned**

- Product requirements and interview flow design
- Prompt strategy for different interview types
- Feedback structure and evaluation rubrics
- Validation approach for AI-generated scoring
- Roadmap thinking across interview modes and learning recommendations

**Product questions explored**

- How should a candidate move through a realistic interview sequence?
- What feedback is useful immediately after an answer?
- How do we keep AI evaluation consistent across SQL, Python, behavioral, and product-style interviews?
- When should the system score, explain, coach, or route the user forward?
